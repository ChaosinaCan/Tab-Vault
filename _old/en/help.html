What is Tab Vault?

Tab Vault is a place to store the tabs you want to get to later without leaving them loaded in memory and on your tab bar. Just like with Opera's tab bar, you can reorder and group tabs and restore deleted tabs from Tab Vault's trash list. Tab Vault also shows you the number of tabs you have stored to remind you to get back to them.

How do I use Tab Vault?

Click the extension button to open Tab Vault. Tab Vault shows the number of tabs it has on this button. This (and many other things) can be changed from the options page.

The Tab Vault Interface

Tab Vault has two lists: Tabs and Trash. Tabs holds your saved tabs. Trash keeps deleted tabs in case you want to restore them. Click the Tabs and Trash links at the top of the popup to change between the lists. (The trash list is disabled if it is empty)

The Tabs List Managing and Opening Tabs

Click Save Tab to save the current tab. Click and drag tabs to reorder them. Click a tab's X button to delete it. You can also delete a tab by dragging it to Trash at the top. The tabs list only shows tab titles, but you can see a tab's URL by hovering your mouse over the tab.

There are three ways to open a tab. Simply click a tab to open it in a new page. Hold Ctrl while clicking a tab to open it in a background page. Drag a tab from the popup and drop it on the web page to open it in the current page.

The Trash List Restoring Deleted Tabs

When you delete a tab, it goes here. Click on a trashed tab to restore it to the tabs list. Click Clear List of Closed Tabs to permanently delete all the trashed tabs. You cannot reorder trashed tabs.

Tab Groups

Tab Vault now supports tab groups. Groups have an expander arrow in place of a favicon and they don't have a X button. To create a group, start dragging a tab and move your cursor over the arrow on the right of the tab. Now, drag the tab over another tab so that the other tab darkens. Finally, drop the tab to group the two tabs together. Note that the cursor must be over the right side of the tab to activate grouping. This grouping area is boxed in red below.

Click on the title of a tab group to expand or collapse it. To edit a group's name, click the pen icon to the right of the title. Tab groups can be dragged and moved just like tabs, and you can drag tabs in and out of expanded groups. A group will disappear when the last tab in the group is deleted or moved outside the group. You can also delete an entire group by dragging it to Trash at the top.

Tab Vault's tab group implementation is designed to be very similar to Opera's tab stacking. Dropping a group onto a tab will merge the group into a new group under the tab. Dropping a group onto another collapsed group will merge the first into the second. Dropping a tab onto a collapsed group will add the tab to the group.

Extra Features

Sessions

Tab Vault can import and export Opera session files from the Import/Export section of the options page.

To export your vault's tabs as a session file, click "Export Session". Opera will open a new tab. Use Menu > Page > Save As... (or Ctrl+S) to save this tab as a .win file.

To import a session file, first open the .win file in a text editor and copy everything from the file into the text box on the options page, then click "Import Session".

You can find where Opera keeps your session files by going to opera:about and reading next to "Saved session". If you want your exported sessions to appear in Opera's session list, save them to this location.

While I have designed Tab Vault so that it is very difficult for any bug to cause catastrophic data loss, I have gotten (very rare) reports of people's tabs completely disappearing from Tab Vault. This could be because Opera lost Tab Vault's data after a crash, a bug in Tab Vault I haven't discovered, or something else entirely. In any case, I recommend you periodically export your tabs for safekeeping, just in case something does go wrong.
